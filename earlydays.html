Rise of the Aircraft Carrier

Early Stages

During World War I, naval aviation was seen as a way to counter the threat posed by German U-boats to Allied shipping. Aircraft were used for reconnaissance, anti-submarine patrols, and attacking submarines.

By providing aerial reconnaissance and escort for convoys, aircraft disrupted U-boat operations and helped protect vital shipping lanes.

The success of naval aviation in Anti-Submarine Warfare operations underscored the need for dedicated aircraft carriers.

Inspired by the British achievements, the United States Navy converted the USS Langley, originally a collier ship, into its first aircraft carrier in 1920.

Extensive modifications were made to the Langley, including the removal of its superstructure to make room for a flight deck and the construction of a hangar deck below to house the aircraft.

The conversion of Langley paved the way for the development of purpose-built aircraft carriers, such as the USS Lexington and USS Saratoga, which became integral to the fleet.
